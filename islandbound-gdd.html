01 Game overview
Concept
Islandbound is a shared-world crafting and exploration game on Roblox. Players start on a single block with nothing but a tree and basic tools. By harvesting resources, processing them through buildings, and depositing them at the island edge, they physically expand the island block by block — unlocking new resources, buildings, and eventually new locations to explore.
Inspired by Dreamdale's island-building loop, built for Roblox's multiplayer audience. All players share the same map and see each other, but progression, inventory, and resources are fully client-sided.
Design pillars
Harvest & craft
No crafting menus. Feed resources into physical buildings, collect from the output side.
Expand & explore
Deposit resources at the island edge to grow your map. Progress toward new biomes and dungeons.
Level & unlock
Every action earns XP. Leveling grants tools, ranks, and perks. Map expansion is driven by deposits, not levels.
Shared world
All players coexist on the same map. No private islands. No shared storage — fully client-sided.

02 Core gameplay loop
Main loop
Harvest resource nodes→ Feed into building→ Collect processed goods→ Walk to island edge→ New block unlocks↩
Resources deposit automatically when the player walks near the island edge — no UI confirmation. The island physically grows. Each new block has a unique, hand-designed resource composition and quantity.
Production loop
No crafting menus exist anywhere in the game. Players carry resources to the building's input side; processed goods auto-collect by proximity on the output side.
Carry resource to building→ Drop on input side→ Building auto-processes→ Walk near output to collect
Session feel
Short session · 5–15 min
Harvest nodes, process at a building, deposit at the edge, unlock 1–2 new blocks, complete a quest.
Long session · 30–60 min
Build a new structure, level up, unlock a larger island section, visit a travel location, trade with the merchant.

05 Progression system
Map expansion is driven by resource deposits at the island edge — not by player level. Level is a separate progression layer. Specific level unlock rewards are still being designed.
Map expansion
Each island block has a resource cost — a specific set of materials the player must deposit at the edge. When the cost is met, the block rises from the water. Block costs are hand-designed and scaled to available resources at that map stage.

06 World & map design
Starting island
Preset, hand-designed map of 35–40 expandable blocks. Every block has a unique resource composition. Players choose expansion direction freely; all paths complete the same final island.
Zone 1
Origin
First blocks. Trees, stone, coal. Sawmill + furnace. Core loop established.
Zone 2
Farmlands
Farm plots, kitchen, fishing tiles, blacksmith. Hoe + fishing rod unlock.
Zone 3
Industrial
Advanced buildings, storage, trading post, mineshaft entrance, dock.
Zone 4
Gateway
Portal, high-tier deposits, final island blocks, end-game prep.
Shared world rules
All players on the same server map can see each other. Inventory, progression, and resources are fully client-sided. One player's harvesting does not deplete nodes for others. Nodes respawn on a timer. There is no shared storage of any kind.
07 Travel & locations
Travel methods
Dock
Zone 3. Sail to other islands — Jungle, Ice, Volcano, and more.
Portal
Zone 4. Instanced dungeons with floors, chests, mobs, and a boss.
Mineshaft

Traveled locations are non-expandable, pre-built maps for resource gathering and combat only. Players return to the main island to use what they find.

08 Combat system
Overview
Melee combat only. Occurs exclusively in traveled locations — the starting island is always peaceful. Players equip a weapon at the blacksmith before entering any combat zone. Dying respawns the player on the main island; backpack resources retained.

09 Social & economy
Currency
Coins — soft currency
Earned through quests, selling to the merchant, defeating mobs. Used for purchases, upgrades, and building costs.
Shards — rare currency
Dropped by bosses and found in dungeons. Used for high-tier crafting and premium blueprints.
Merchant
Traveling NPC. Visits twice per day at randomized times with a randomized inventory. Players buy rare items and blueprints, or sell surplus resources for coins. Primary resource sink in the economy.
Trading post
Unlocked in Zone 3. Players list resources for others to buy or swap. Creates a lightweight player-driven economy on the shared server.
Pets & events
Pets follow the player and provide passive bonuses. Earned through milestones, events, and promo codes. Exclusive cosmetic skins may be Robux-purchasable. Seasonal events are free to all players regardless of level.

10 Monetization
All gameplay content is free and earnable through play. Paid items are cosmetic, QoL, or time-saving — never pay-to-win. No ads. No paywalled progression.
